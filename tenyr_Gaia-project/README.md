# Ten(+) Years of Binaries in Gaia
**Author:** Francesco Guerriero  
**Supervisors:** Prof. Brown, Dr. Penoyre  
**Institution:** Leiden University, Astronomy Department  
**Degree:** MSc in Astronomy Research | Academic Year 2024/2025  

## Overview
This is the extended research project thesis that forms the foundation for the published paper *"Nominal thresholds for good astrometric fits, and prospects for binary detectability, for the full extended Gaia mission"* (Guerriero et al., 2026). While the paper focuses on thresholds and detectability prospects, this thesis provides the complete methodological framework, detailed mathematical derivations, comprehensive results across multiple data releases (DR1–DR5), and an in-depth analysis of the parallax bias phenomenon in Gaia astrometry.

## Key insights expanded in this thesis:
-**Parallax bias analysis**: The thesis reveals that Gaia exhibits a systematic parallax offset peaking at periods close to the observational baseline. This effect grows more pronounced in later data releases and is confirmed against Gaia DR3 Non-Single Star (NSS) catalogue observations.

-**Brown dwarf and low-mass companion detectability**: Analysis of systems hosting a companion less massive than 80 M_Jupiter shows dramatic improvement from earlier to later data releases (~10% detection in DR2 to ~80% in DR5 for P ~ 10 yr systems).
